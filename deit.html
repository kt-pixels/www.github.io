    Weight gain is a gradual process that requires a combination of a balanced diet, proper exercise, and consistency. It's important to focus on healthy weight gain rather than just increasing calorie intake. Here's a sample weight gain diet chart that you can follow. Remember, it's always a good idea to consult with a healthcare professional or a registered dietitian before making significant changes to your diet.

    Please remember that all these diet plans are examples, and you can create a good diet plan for yourself using them as a reference. If you want me to create a diet plan for you, you can contact me using the provided contact links below.

    5. Vegetarian or Vegan Diet

    Time Meal Food Items
    1. Morning (7:00 AM - 8:00 AM) Breakfast
    • Scrambled Tofu with Vegetables: Cook tofu with bell peppers, onions, and spinach, seasoned with turmeric and nutritional yeast.
    • Whole Grain Toast: 2 slices of whole-grain bread.
    • Fresh Fruit Salad: A mix of your favorite fruits like apples, berries, and bananas.
    2. Mid-Morning (10:00 AM) Snacks
    • Greek Yogurt (for vegetarians) or Coconut Yogurt (for vegans): A small cup for some probiotics and protein.
    • Almonds or Walnuts: A small handful for healthy fats.
    3. Lunch (12:30 PM - 1:30 PM) Lunch
    • Chickpea Salad: Combine chickpeas with cucumber, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and a lemon-tahini dressing.
    • Brown Rice: A serving of brown rice or quinoa for complex carbs.
    • Steamed Broccoli: A side of steamed broccoli for added fiber and nutrients.
    4. Afternoon (3:00 PM) Snacks
    • Carrot Sticks with Hummus: A great combination for crunch and flavor.
    • Green Tea or Herbal Tea: A cup of your choice for antioxidants.
    5. Evening Snack (5:00 PM) Snacks
    • A piece of fruit or a small handful of mixed berries.
    6. Dinner (8:00 PM) Dinner
    • Lentil Curry: Prepare a hearty lentil curry with plenty of spices, tomatoes, and greens.
    • Quinoa or Whole Wheat Roti (Indian flatbread): Serve the curry with either of these options.
    • Steamed Asparagus: A side of steamed asparagus for added vitamins.
    7. Before Bed (10:00 PM) Sleeping Time
    • A glass of warm almond milk or herbal tea for a soothing end to the day.

    6. Heart-Healthy Diet

    Time Meal Food Items
    1. Morning (7:00 AM - 8:00 AM) Breakfast
    • Option 1: Oatmeal topped with fresh berries and a sprinkle of flaxseeds.
    • Option 2: Whole-grain toast with almond butter and sliced banana.
    • A glass of freshly squeezed orange juice.
    2. Mid-Morning (10:00 AM) Snacks
    • A small handful of unsalted mixed nuts (like almonds, walnuts, and pistachios).
    • A piece of fruit (apple, pear, or berries).
    3. Lunch (12:30 PM - 1:30 PM) Lunch
    • Grilled skinless chicken breast or tofu for a vegetarian option.
    • A generous portion of mixed greens, including spinach and kale.
    • Quinoa or brown rice with steamed vegetables.
    • A light vinaigrette dressing made with olive oil and balsamic vinegar.
    4. Afternoon (3:00 - 3:30 PM) Snacks
    • Greek yogurt with honey and a sprinkle of chia seeds.
    • Carrot and cucumber sticks with hummus for dipping.
    5. Dinner (6:30 -7:30 PM) Dinner
    • Baked or broiled salmon (rich in omega-3 fatty acids). (OPTIONAL : - FISH OIL CAPSULES)
    • Steamed broccoli and asparagus.
    • Mashed sweet potatoes seasoned with a touch of cinnamon.
    • A side salad with mixed greens and a simple olive oil and lemon dressing.
    6. Before Bed (10:00 PM) Sleeping Time
    • A small bowl of mixed berries or a piece of dark chocolate (70% cocoa or higher)..
